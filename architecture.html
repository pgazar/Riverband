Stop the hand-texting: how the volunteer reminder system would work

Today, sign-ups scatter across a form, a sheet, and Airtable, and every reminder is texted by hand. This is the same information flowing through one roster, with reminders sent automatically — and a dashboard Diane can check any time.

Already paid for (Airtable, Google, Slack) The automation (Claude + MCP) New cost, needs approval (texting service) People & their phones

What it costs, what it saves

Priya’s time back
~312 hrs/year
Roughly 6 hours a week of manual re-entry and texting — about $6,000–8,000/year in staff time.
The only new expense
~$45/year
The texting service is the single item we don’t already own. Everything else runs on tools we already pay for.
ILLUSTRATIVE
Possible volunteer-hour upside
~$29,000/year
If night-before reminders cut no-shows even modestly (say 35% → 20%), that’s the recovered value of volunteer hours at the $33/hr Independent Sector rate. Our current no-show rate is anecdotal and untracked — this is an illustration of the potential, not a promise.
Bottom line for Diane: ~$45 a year buys back ~6 hours of Priya’s week, replaces three scattered lists with one roster, and gives the board a live signups-by-shift dashboard. The one decision needed is approving the texting service.
Figures grounded in the team handbook (Priya’s ~6 hrs/week; Independent Sector rate ~$33/hr; no-show rate noted as anecdotal). The ~$29,000 figure is explicitly illustrative. Airtable stays the single source of truth; nothing here changes volunteer eligibility or age rules.
